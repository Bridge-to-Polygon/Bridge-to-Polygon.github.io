What is Bridge to Polygon?

Polygon Portal is the official Polygon interface for bridging, swapping, and managing assets on Polygon, linked from the Polygon site. Use it to connect a wallet, choose a route, and send supported tokens to Polygon PoS.

How Bridge to Polygon works

  1. Connect walletOpen Polygon Portal and connect the wallet holding the source-chain funds.
  2. Choose routeSelect the source network, Polygon PoS as destination, and a supported token.
  3. Review promptsCheck token, network, recipient wallet, gas, and approvals before signing.
  4. Track transferAfter confirmation, follow status in the portal or verify activity on Polygonscan.

Before you bridge

Official entry point

Polygon lists Portal as the place to bridge, swap, and manage assets on Polygon, so the primary action should route to the verified app domain.

Clear route review

A careful flow separates source chain, destination network, token, gas, and wallet prompts before the transfer is signed.

Explorer-ready

When a Polygon-side transaction is available, users can verify it on Polygonscan.

Bridge to Polygon FAQ

How do I bridge to polygon from Ethereum?

Open Polygon Portal, connect your wallet, choose Ethereum as the source and Polygon PoS as the destination, then review the route and wallet prompts before signing.

Is bridge to polygon an official Polygon app?

The official destination is Polygon Portal at portal.polygon.technology/bridge; verify the domain before connecting a wallet.

What wallet do I need to bridge to polygon?

Use a compatible self-custody wallet that supports the source network and Polygon PoS, and make sure it has enough native gas for the transaction.

How long does bridge to polygon take?

Timing depends on the route, token, network congestion, and wallet confirmations; rely on the portal status and explorer records rather than fixed estimates.

What should I check before I bridge to polygon?

Confirm the official domain, token, destination network, wallet address, approvals, and gas details; bridges involve smart-contract and operational risk.
