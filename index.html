What is Bridge to Polygon?

Polygon Portal is the official Polygon interface for bridging, swapping, and managing assets on Polygon, linked from the Polygon site. Use it to connect a wallet, choose a route, and send supported tokens to Polygon PoS.

Bridge to Polygon fees and gas

Bridge to Polygon fees depend on Ethereum gas for deposits, Polygon gas for destination transactions, and any ERC-20 approval required before the bridge transfer. Polygon PoS transactions are usually low cost, but the first Ethereum-side deposit can still be expensive during busy mainnet periods. Users should keep enough POL on Polygon for later transactions or bridge/swap into gas after arrival. For the official route, start from Polygon Portal Bridge and verify the received token on PolygonScan.

How long does Polygon bridge take?

Deposits from Ethereum to Polygon PoS are usually much faster than exits back to Ethereum, because withdrawals involve Polygon checkpointing and finalization steps. Exact timing depends on the asset, route, and network conditions. Many users choose the official Polygon bridge for canonical asset movement and a third-party liquidity bridge when speed matters more than canonical exit mechanics. If a transfer appears delayed, check the Ethereum transaction hash first, then the Polygon destination address and token contract.

Polygon bridge supported tokens

Polygon bridge routes commonly support ETH, POL-related assets, stablecoins, and many ERC-20 tokens, but support is contract-specific. A token can have more than one representation on Polygon, especially if it has been bridged by different providers or migrated over time. For DeFi use, the contract recognized by the destination app is the key detail. Before transferring a large amount, confirm the exact Polygon token address, not just the ticker, and consider a small test transfer for assets with multiple versions.

Bridge MATIC or POL to Polygon

Polygon's ecosystem has moved from MATIC toward POL branding and token infrastructure, but users may still see both names across wallets, exchanges, and older documentation. When bridging to Polygon, check whether the route expects POL, legacy MATIC, or another gas asset representation. The destination wallet should be set to Polygon PoS, and the token contract should match current Polygon ecosystem guidance. Avoid sending tokens to unsupported legacy routes without checking official docs or exchange network labels.

Bridge to Polygon FAQ

What gas token do I need on Polygon?

Polygon PoS uses POL for gas in current ecosystem naming, although many wallets and older resources may still display MATIC labels. Keep a small amount available before using bridged tokens. Without gas, your assets can arrive but you may not be able to approve, swap, or transfer them.

Can I bridge USDC to Polygon?

Yes, USDC is commonly used on Polygon, but users should distinguish native USDC from older bridged representations where relevant. The destination protocol's supported contract matters. If a DeFi app lists a specific USDC version, bridge or swap into that exact version rather than assuming all USDC tickers are interchangeable.

Why is my Polygon bridge withdrawal slow?

Withdrawals from Polygon to Ethereum can require checkpointing and finalization, so they can take longer than deposits. A slow withdrawal is not automatically a failed transaction. Check the source transaction, the bridge status, and the Ethereum finalization requirement before submitting another transaction.

Is Polygon Portal the official bridge?

Polygon Portal is the official interface for Polygon ecosystem bridging and related wallet actions. Third-party bridges may also support Polygon, but they use their own liquidity, contracts, and routing. Use the official portal when canonical asset movement is more important than the fastest quote.

Can I bridge NFTs to Polygon?

NFT bridging depends on the NFT contract, bridge support, and marketplace recognition. Do not assume an ERC-721 or ERC-1155 can move through the same route as an ERC-20 token. For NFTs, use project-specific guidance or a bridge that explicitly lists the collection and destination contract.

Do I need MetaMask configured for Polygon?

Yes, an EVM wallet needs Polygon PoS network settings to display and use bridged assets. Many apps can add the network automatically, but manual setup should use official Polygon docs or trusted wallet prompts. After bridging, verify the address on PolygonScan if the wallet balance does not refresh.
